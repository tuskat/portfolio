## Remaining issues

### 1. "Grew from junior to most senior engineer" — reads a bit self-assessed
This is true and important, but it's the kind of claim that's stronger coming from someone else (George's rec hints at it). As a self-written bullet it can read as unverifiable. Consider reframing as a fact rather than a judgement:
> "Sole remaining engineer from the original team by end of tenure, having onboarded and mentored multiple junior developers"

Or just leave it — the 7.5 years speaks for itself and the rec backs it up.

### 2. ExpressVPN entry has only one bullet and it says nothing
"Continued work... details subject to NDA" is honest but leaves a gap. You can say *something* without breaching NDA — the category of work, the scale, the type of problem. Even:
> "Continued building and iterating on the full ExpressVPN web estate — CMS tooling, customer-facing pages, and internal platforms — across a globally distributed team"

The NDA covers specifics, not the general nature of the work.

### 3. NLS / FLO Capital — no bullets, no context on what you actually did
It's a short role (8 months) so it doesn't need much, but right now it's just a stack. Even one line would help:
> "Built the investor-facing frontend for an early-stage private equity crowdfunding platform"

### 4. Intralogue — same issue, no bullets
One line is enough:
> "Developed core UI components for a collaborative workspace startup at an early product stage"

### 5. Skills grid — ordering could be more strategic
Currently: JS/TS, Vue, React/Angular, Astro, Node/Express, Rails, Flutter, .NET/C#/Unity

Your primary identity is Vue + React + TypeScript. Rails is deep experience. Flutter/Unity are side projects. Consider grouping or ordering by relevance to the roles you're targeting, or at minimum putting TypeScript first since it's what senior roles filter on.

### 6. "Front-End Developer" vs "Frontend Developer" inconsistency
NLS and Intralogue use "Front-End Developer" (hyphenated). The rest uses "Frontend". Pick one — unhyphenated is current industry convention.

### 7. Phone number format
`+34 60 70 23 935` — Spanish numbers are 9 digits after the country code, formatted as `+34 607 023 935`. Current formatting looks slightly off.

### 8. Mark Ray-Smith's rec — missing company name
His title says "Global Head of Architecture, Securities Services" but no company. Add it if possible — without context it floats. This was presumably HSBC or similar, which would add significant weight.
